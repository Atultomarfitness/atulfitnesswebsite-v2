Lying Leg Curl

Build Stronger Hamstrings with Better Strength, Stability, and Muscle Definition

The Lying Leg Curl is one of the most effective isolation exercises for strengthening and developing the hamstring muscles. Performed on a lying leg curl machine, this exercise trains knee flexion through a controlled range of motion while minimizing lower-back involvement. It's an excellent choice for increasing hamstring strength, improving athletic performance, supporting knee stability, reducing injury risk, and building balanced lower-body muscle development.

Step-by-Step Technique

How to Perform the Lying Leg Curl

Follow these step-by-step instructions to perform the Lying Leg Curl with proper machine setup, controlled movement, and full hamstring activation to maximize muscle growth, improve knee flexion strength, and reduce the risk of injury.

STEP 01

Adjust the Machine

Lie face down on the leg curl machine with your knees aligned with the machine's pivot point. Position the roller pad comfortably against the back of your lower legs just above your heels.

Coach Tip

Proper machine alignment ensures smooth movement and maximum hamstring activation.

STEP 02

Curl Your Legs Up

Exhale as you bend your knees and curl the pad toward your glutes. Keep your hips firmly against the bench throughout the movement.

Coach Tip

Lift the weight smoothly without using momentum or raising your hips.

STEP 03

Squeeze the Hamstrings

Pause briefly at the top of the movement while squeezing your hamstrings as hard as possible before beginning the lowering phase.

Coach Tip

A one-second squeeze improves the mind-muscle connection and increases muscle activation.

STEP 04

Lower Under Control

Slowly lower the weight back to the starting position until your legs are almost fully extended, maintaining constant tension on the hamstrings throughout the movement.

Coach Tip

Control the lowering phase instead of letting the weight stack pull your legs down.

STEP 05

Repeat with Proper Form

Complete the desired number of repetitions using a slow, controlled tempo. Keep your hips against the bench and maintain consistent hamstring tension throughout every repetition.

Coach Tip

Master perfect technique before increasing the weight to maximize results and reduce injury risk.

Avoid These Errors

Common Lying Leg Curl Mistakes

Avoid these common technique mistakes to maximize hamstring development, protect your knees, and perform the Lying Leg Curl safely and effectively.

01

Using Momentum to Lift the Weight

Swinging the weight or jerking the movement reduces hamstring activation and places unnecessary stress on the knees and lower back.

How to Fix It

Curl and lower the weight slowly while keeping constant tension on the hamstrings.

02

Lifting Your Hips Off the Pad

Raising your hips to move heavier weight reduces hamstring isolation and shifts stress to the lower back.

How to Fix It

Keep your hips firmly against the bench throughout every repetition.

03

Incorrect Machine Setup

Poor pad placement or incorrect knee alignment changes the movement mechanics, reduces muscle activation, and may cause discomfort.

How to Fix It

Align your knees with the machine's pivot point and position the roller pad just above your heels before each set.

04

Using Excessive Weight

Choosing more weight than you can control shortens the range of motion, encourages poor technique, and reduces hamstring engagement.

How to Fix It

Select a weight that allows smooth, controlled repetitions with a full range of motion and proper technique.

Frequently Asked Questions

Lying Leg Curl FAQ

Find answers to common questions about Lying Leg Curl technique, muscles worked, knee safety, training frequency, and how to maximize hamstring development with this effective isolation exercise.

What muscles does the Lying Leg Curl work?

The Lying Leg Curl primarily targets the hamstrings, including the biceps femoris, semitendinosus, and semimembranosus. The gastrocnemius also assists during knee flexion.

Is the Lying Leg Curl safe for the knees?

Yes. When performed with proper machine setup, controlled movement, and an appropriate weight, the Lying Leg Curl is generally safe for healthy individuals. Avoid using momentum or excessive resistance.

Should beginners perform Lying Leg Curls?

Yes. The Lying Leg Curl is beginner-friendly because the machine provides stability, making it easier to learn proper hamstring activation and movement control.

How many sets and reps should I perform?

For muscle growth, perform 3–4 sets of 10–15 repetitions. For muscular endurance, use lighter weights for 15–20 repetitions while maintaining strict technique.

Should I lift my hips during the exercise?

No. Keep your hips firmly against the bench throughout the movement. Lifting your hips reduces hamstring isolation and may place unnecessary stress on your lower back.

When should I include Lying Leg Curls in my workout?

Lying Leg Curls are commonly performed after compound exercises like squats or Romanian deadlifts to fully fatigue the hamstrings. They also work well as a focused hamstring isolation exercise in any leg workout.
