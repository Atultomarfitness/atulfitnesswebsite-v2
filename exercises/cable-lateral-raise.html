Cable Lateral Raise

Build Wider Shoulders, Maintain Constant Tension & Maximize Side Deltoid Growth

The Cable Lateral Raise is an isolation shoulder exercise that primarily targets the lateral deltoids while also engaging the anterior deltoids, supraspinatus, upper trapezius, and core stabilizers. Unlike dumbbells, cables provide constant tension throughout the entire range of motion, helping maximize muscle activation, build broader shoulders, improve shoulder definition, and promote balanced upper-body development.

Step-by-Step Technique

How to Perform the Cable Lateral Raise

Follow these step-by-step instructions to perform the Cable Lateral Raise with proper posture, controlled movement, and constant cable tension to maximize lateral deltoid activation and build broader, more defined shoulders.

STEP 01

Set the Cable and Starting Position

Attach a single handle to the lowest pulley of a cable machine. Stand beside the machine, grasp the handle with the outside hand, and position the cable slightly across the front of your body with a slight bend in your elbow.

Coach Tip

Step slightly away from the machine to create constant tension on the cable before beginning the movement.

STEP 02

Brace Your Core

Tighten your core, lift your chest, and maintain a neutral spine. Keep your body stable without leaning toward or away from the cable machine.

Coach Tip

Keep your torso still throughout the exercise so the movement comes only from your shoulder joint.

STEP 03

Raise the Handle to Shoulder Height

Lift the cable handle out to your side in a smooth arc until your upper arm reaches approximately shoulder height. Maintain a slight bend in your elbow and avoid using momentum.

Coach Tip

Lead with your elbow rather than your hand to keep maximum tension on the lateral deltoid.

STEP 04

Pause While Maintaining Cable Tension

Briefly pause at shoulder height while keeping constant tension on the cable. Avoid shrugging your shoulders or lifting the handle higher than shoulder level.

Coach Tip

Focus on squeezing your side deltoid instead of using your upper traps to finish the movement.

STEP 05

Lower the Handle Slowly

Slowly lower the cable handle back to the starting position while maintaining control and continuous tension throughout the entire lowering phase. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions before switching sides.

Coach Tip

Don't allow the weight stack to rest between repetitions. Keeping constant tension helps maximize lateral deltoid activation.

Avoid These Errors

Common Cable Lateral Raise Mistakes

Avoid these common technique mistakes to maximize lateral deltoid activation, maintain constant cable tension, and perform the Cable Lateral Raise safely and effectively.

01

Using Body Momentum

Swinging your torso or using momentum to lift the cable reduces lateral deltoid activation and shifts the workload away from the target muscles.

How to Fix It

Use a lighter weight, brace your core, and raise the handle with slow, controlled movement while keeping your body stable.

02

Shrugging the Shoulders

Elevating your shoulders during the raise causes the upper trapezius to dominate the movement and reduces emphasis on the lateral deltoids.

How to Fix It

Keep your shoulders down and relaxed while leading the movement with your elbow instead of lifting with your upper traps.

03

Raising the Handle Too High

Lifting the cable well above shoulder height increases trap involvement without providing additional stimulation to the lateral deltoids.

How to Fix It

Raise the handle until your upper arm is roughly parallel to the floor while maintaining smooth, controlled movement.

04

Letting the Weight Stack Rest

Allowing the weight stack to touch down between repetitions removes cable tension and reduces the effectiveness of the exercise.

How to Fix It

Keep constant tension on the cable throughout the entire set by controlling both the lifting and lowering phases without resting the weight stack.

Expert Coaching Insights

Coach's Tips

Apply these practical coaching cues to improve your Cable Lateral Raise technique, maintain constant cable tension, maximize side deltoid activation, and perform every repetition with greater control and precision.

01

Lead with Your Elbow

Initiate the movement by lifting your elbow rather than your hand. This keeps the emphasis on the lateral deltoid and improves shoulder isolation.

Remember

Think about driving your elbow upward while your hand simply follows the cable's path.

02

Maintain Constant Cable Tension

One of the biggest advantages of cables is continuous resistance. Keep tension on the working shoulder throughout the entire repetition.

Remember

Avoid resting at the bottom or letting the weight stack touch down between repetitions.

03

Keep Your Shoulders Down

Relax your shoulders throughout the movement to reduce upper trapezius involvement and keep the lateral deltoids doing the majority of the work.

Remember

Keep your shoulders away from your ears while maintaining a smooth, controlled raising motion.

04

Prioritize Control Over Weight

The Cable Lateral Raise is an isolation exercise that responds best to strict technique. Lighter loads with perfect control are more effective than heavy weights lifted with momentum.

Remember

Focus on feeling your side deltoid working through every inch of the movement before increasing the resistance.

Build Strength Step by Step

Cable Lateral Raise Progression

Progress from learning proper cable lateral raise mechanics to building stronger side deltoids, improving shoulder stability, and advancing to more challenging shoulder isolation variations while maintaining constant cable tension.

Stage 01
Foundation

Master Proper Cable Raise Technique

Start with a light cable resistance to learn correct body positioning, cable alignment, shoulder control, and movement mechanics before increasing the load.

Main Focus

Strict form, stable posture, relaxed shoulders, controlled tempo, and maintaining constant cable tension throughout the movement.

Stage 02
Control

Build Consistent Side Deltoid Strength

Perform controlled repetitions with moderate resistance while keeping continuous cable tension and minimizing body movement throughout every repetition.

Main Focus

Constant tension, smooth repetitions, shoulder stability, controlled tempo, and balanced muscle activation.

Stage 03
Strength & Development

Increase Cable Resistance Progressively

Gradually increase the cable resistance while maintaining strict technique, full range of motion, and continuous tension on the lateral deltoids.

Main Focus

Progressive overload, constant cable tension, controlled movement, shoulder stability, and perfect technique under heavier resistance.

Stage 04
Performance

Advance to More Challenging Raise Variations

After mastering the Cable Lateral Raise, progress to advanced variations such as the Behind-the-Back Cable Lateral Raise, Leaning Cable Lateral Raise, Single-Arm Cable Raise, or Machine Lateral Raise to continue improving shoulder size and definition.

Main Focus

Greater shoulder isolation, continuous tension, improved muscle symmetry, progressive overload, and choosing advanced variations that match your training goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Cable Lateral Raise FAQ

Find clear answers to common questions about the Cable Lateral Raise, including proper technique, muscles worked, cable setup, shoulder position, training frequency, and progression.

What muscles does the Cable Lateral Raise work?

The Cable Lateral Raise primarily targets the lateral deltoids while also engaging the anterior deltoids, supraspinatus, upper trapezius, and core stabilizers. The cable provides continuous tension throughout the entire range of motion.

Is the Cable Lateral Raise better than the Dumbbell Lateral Raise?

Both exercises are effective. The Cable Lateral Raise provides constant resistance throughout the movement, while the Dumbbell Lateral Raise relies on gravity and has less tension at the bottom. Including both variations can help maximize shoulder development.

How high should I raise the cable handle?

Raise the handle until your upper arm is approximately parallel to the floor. Lifting much higher often increases upper trapezius involvement without providing additional stimulation to the lateral deltoids.

Is the Cable Lateral Raise suitable for beginners?

Yes. Beginners should start with light resistance, focus on strict technique, avoid using momentum, and gradually increase the weight as shoulder strength and movement control improve.

How often should I do the Cable Lateral Raise?

Most people benefit from performing the Cable Lateral Raise one to three times per week as part of a balanced shoulder or upper-body training program while allowing sufficient recovery between sessions.

How many sets and reps should I do for the Cable Lateral Raise?

The ideal number of sets and repetitions depends on your training goals and experience. For general muscle growth, performing 2–4 working sets of 10–15 controlled repetitions while maintaining constant cable tension and strict technique is a common and effective recommendation.
